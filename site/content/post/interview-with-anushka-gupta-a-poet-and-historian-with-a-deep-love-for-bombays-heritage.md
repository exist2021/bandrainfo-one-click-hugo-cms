Anushka Gupta, describes herself as a poet and historian who has a deep love for Bombay's heritage. She photographs and writes about heritage buildings, and the lesser known hidden gems in the city, with an endeavour to revive the cultural and historical essence of Bombay.

You should follow her on [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/anushkagupta5/) where she documents oral histories, and write stories of the people inhabiting these heritage spaces, tracing origins and family history.

> Below are some questions I pitch to her to know more about her life in Bandra and her work. 

## How long here you been living in Bandra?

I have been living in Bandra, Mount Mary for the last thirty two years. I was born and brought up here.

## What are your favorite restaurants & hangouts in Bandra? What are your favourite places to shop in Bandra?

Bandra is home, it is where I spend most of my time. The seaside by bandstand, would certainly be a favourite hangout. I love the little cafes sequestered in the bylanes of Bandra.

> The ones I frequent often are Taj Mahal Tea House, The Yoga House, Sequel, Suzette, The Village Shop, Birdsong cafe, Provenance cafe. The cafes have warm and charming spaces that seamlessly integrate the character of the neighbourhood, and make for beautiful corners to read, write, work.To shop : I like anokhi, The Shop at Pali naka, Cottons, and some beautiful bookstores like Trilogy.

## As a historian, which is your favourite part of the city?

As a historian, the Victorian - Gothic part of the city has my heart. The Fort precinct and everything that encircles the once standing Bombay Castle.I love exploring hidden gems and unraveling histories of lesser known buildings. I seek solace in the city's Libraries, that have now been beautifully restored. My absolute favourite building would be the erstwhile Tata Mansion, called the Esplanade House.

## What makes Bandra endearing?

I like exploring the quieter bylanes of the neighbourhood, lined with old cottages and portugese style homes. The villages within bandra are of great interest to me; Ranvar village, Pali village, Chuim village. I enjoy talking to the koli fisher folk and seeing the city through their perspective. The churches of bandra and the crosses on the street corners are always so interesting.

Characteristically quaint Bandra homes, unperturbed by their amorphous neighbours, untouched by the maladies of time. . In conversation with a dear friend, I discovered stories behind the ‘Saudades’. I have always mused over this beautiful rust pink cottage, and wondered about the people behind these wooden doors.  He told me about French lessons at the Saudades, and of everything he imbibed from this house. . It belongs to the Goan family of Desales, a generation of teachers, by profession. Wayne de Desales, an endearing gentleman in his fifties; high waisted pants, pinstriped shirt, mustache and spectacles, with the kindest eyes and gentlest soul, his voice a soft whisper.  Monsieur Desales inherited this home from his grandfather, and takes french lessons for young adults, in a room full of benches and wooden tables, ladies seated on the right hand side, lads on the left. . Fading pink wallpaper on the walls, high ceilings and everything aged and antiquated, the Saudades is a large house, with a little garden full of plants in the back. . ‘Saudade’ in Portuguese translates to a beautiful sadness, a yearning for someone lost to love, life. ## Tell us a Bandra secret?

There are so many bandra secrets, every walk unearths a new one.

> I love the little home near Chapel road, called 'The Nook'. One of my favourite discoveries is the little park between bandra fort and bandstand (near the seaside cafe).
